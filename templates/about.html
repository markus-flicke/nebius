About

A platform where every tool publishes the conditions under which it works and a measured success rate computed from real runs — not a declared one.

Most tools tell you what they claim to do. This platform tells you what they actually do. Each tool lists the conditions for success right next to a success rate that is computed from real executions, so the number reflects reality instead of a promise.

How success rate is measured

Every execution is recorded with a success or failure flag. A tool's success rate is simply successful_runs / total_runs. A tool signals failure by raising an exception from its run() method — so a tool that only works on Wednesdays simply raises on other days, and its measured rate honestly reflects that.

Both a global success rate and your own personal success rate are shown. Identify yourself with ?user=<name> or the X-User header to see how a tool performs for you.

What you can do here

  • Browse tools with their inputs, the conditions under which they work, and their measured success rate.
  • Run a tool by filling in its inputs and watching the result come back live.
  • Add your own tool by submitting a small Python class that describes its inputs, outputs, and the conditions for success.
